# Ancilla-Drive-Enhanced Metrology: Beating the SQL with an Actively Driven Ancilla Particle

## 🧪 Hypothesis

For a system--ancilla pair of single-particle two-mode bosonic systems where the system S couples to the unknown phase $\theta$ via $H_S = \theta J_z^S$, the ancilla A is driven during the holding period by a controllable local Hamiltonian $H_A = a_x J_x^A + a_y J_y^A + a_z J_z^A$, and the system--ancilla interaction is the Ising-type $H_{\text{int}} = a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A$, the sensitivity $\Delta\theta$ (error-propagation uncertainty in estimating $\theta$ via a $J_z^S$ measurement on the system) can beat the standard quantum limit (SQL) $\Delta\theta = 1/T_H$ despite using only $N=1$ particle in the interferometer. The holding time is fixed at $T_H = 10$ for all experiments, giving an SQL reference of $\Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}} = 0.1$.

The central hypothesis decomposes into three specific, testable claims:

1. **SQL violation**: There exist finite values of $(a_x, a_y, a_z, a_{xx})$ and $\theta$ such that $\Delta\theta < 1/T_H$, i.e., the sensitivity surpasses the $N=1$ SQL.

2. **Essential role of the ancilla drive**: The SQL violation requires all three components: the ancilla drive $H_A \neq 0$ (at least one of $a_x, a_y, a_z$ non-zero), the system--ancilla interaction $a_{xx} \neq 0$, and the presence of the system phase $H_S \neq 0$. The drive-free limit ($a_x = a_y = a_z = 0$) recovers $\Delta\theta \geq 1/T_H$, consistent with prior work that found no improvement with a passive ancilla.

3. **Optimal drive configuration**: The optimal ancilla drive involves at least one non-commuting generator: when $[H_A, J_z^A] \neq 0$ (requiring $a_x \neq 0$ or $a_y \neq 0$), the time-dependent $J_z^A(t)$ generated by $H_A$ creates an effective time-dependent Hamiltonian $H_{\text{eff}}(t) = \theta J_z^S + a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A(t)$ whose non-commuting structure at different times can enhance information encoding. A purely $J_z^A$-drive ($a_x = a_y = 0$, $a_z \neq 0$) is expected to be insufficient.

**Null hypothesis**: No combination of $(a_x, a_y, a_z, a_{xx})$ can produce $\Delta\theta < 1/T_H$. The ancilla drive, even when optimized, cannot overcome the fundamental $J=1/2$ spectral radius bound on the system.

## ⚛️ Theoretical Model

The total Hilbert space is $\mathcal{H}_{\text{tot}} = \mathcal{H}_S \otimes \mathcal{H}_A$, where each subsystem is a **two-mode bosonic Fock space** truncated at one particle per mode. The single-particle sector $\mathcal{H}_{1} = \text{span}\{\vert1,0\rangle,\, \vert0,1\rangle\}$ (dimension 2) is isomorphic to a spin-$1/2$, and the full space has dimension 4 with ordered computational basis $\{\vert00\rangle, \vert01\rangle, \vert10\rangle, \vert11\rangle\}$ where $\vert0\rangle = \vert1,0\rangle$ (particle in mode 0) and $\vert1\rangle = \vert0,1\rangle$ (particle in mode 1). The **angular momentum operators** for each subsystem satisfy SU(2) algebra $[J_i, J_j] = i \epsilon_{ijk} J_k$ and are represented by $J_k = \sigma_k/2$ (the $2\times2$ Pauli matrices). These are embedded into the full space via Kronecker products: $J_k^S = \sigma_k/2 \otimes \mathbb{1}_2$ and $J_k^A = \mathbb{1}_2 \otimes \sigma_k/2$.

The **initial state** is a pure product state $\vert\Psi_0\rangle = \vert1,0\rangle_S \otimes \vert1,0\rangle_A$, which is $\vert00\rangle$ in the computational basis.

The **circuit protocol** proceeds in four steps:

1. **Beam splitter on system only**: A 50/50 symmetric beam splitter acts on the system, generated by $J_x^S = \sigma_x^S/2$. The unitary is $U_{\text{BS}} = \exp(-i (\pi/2) J_x^S) = \exp(-i (\pi/4) \sigma_x^S)$, which acts as identity on the ancilla: $U_{\text{BS}}^{(S)} = U_{\text{BS}} \otimes \mathbb{1}_2$.

2. **Holding period with simultaneous encoding, ancilla drive, and interaction**: The full state evolves under the total Hamiltonian $H = H_S + H_A + H_{\text{int}}$ for duration $T_H$. The three terms are:
   - $H_S = \theta J_z^S = \frac{\theta}{2} \sigma_z^S \otimes \mathbb{1}_2$ — the unknown phase encoded on the system,
   - $H_A = a_x J_x^A + a_y J_y^A + a_z J_z^A = \mathbb{1}_2 \otimes \left(\frac{a_x}{2} \sigma_x^A + \frac{a_y}{2} \sigma_y^A + \frac{a_z}{2} \sigma_z^A\right)$ — a controllable magnetic field on the ancilla,
   - $H_{\text{int}} = a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A = \frac{a_{xx}}{4} (\sigma_z^S \otimes \sigma_z^A)$ — an Ising-type interaction coupling the system and ancilla.
   The hold unitary is $U_{\text{hold}}(T_H) = \exp(-i T_H H)$.

3. **Beam splitter on system only**: A second 50/50 beam splitter (identical to step 1) acts on the system: $U_{\text{BS}}^{(S)}$.

4. **Measurement**: $J_z^S$ is measured on the system qubit. The expectation value is $\langle J_z^S \rangle = \langle\Psi_{\text{final}}\vert J_z^S \vert\Psi_{\text{final}}\rangle$ and the variance is $\text{Var}(J_z^S) = \langle (J_z^S)^2 \rangle - \langle J_z^S \rangle^2$.

The **complete evolution** is:
$$\vert\Psi_{\text{final}}\rangle = U_{\text{BS}}^{(S)} \, U_{\text{hold}}(T_H) \, U_{\text{BS}}^{(S)} \, \vert\Psi_0\rangle.$$

The **sensitivity** via **error propagation** is:
$$\Delta\theta = \frac{\sqrt{\text{Var}(J_z^S)}}{\vert \partial\langle J_z^S\rangle / \partial\theta \vert},$$
where the derivative is computed via central finite differences with step $\delta = 10^{-6}$. The **standard quantum limit** for $N=1$ particle is $\Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}} = 1/T_H$, corresponding to the maximum QFI $F_Q = T_H^2$ for a single qubit under $J_z$ rotation.

**Physical mechanism**: When $H_A \neq 0$ with at least one non-commuting component ($a_x \neq 0$ or $a_y \neq 0$), the ancilla operator $J_z^A$ acquires time dependence in the interaction picture: $J_z^A(t) = e^{i t H_A} J_z^A e^{-i t H_A}$. The interaction term becomes time-dependent: $H_{\text{int}}(t) = a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A(t)$. This means the system experiences an effective Hamiltonian that does not commute with itself at different times:
$$[J_z^S \otimes J_z^A(t_1),\, J_z^S \otimes J_z^A(t_2)] \neq 0 \quad \text{when } [J_z^A(t_1), J_z^A(t_2)] \neq 0.$$
This non-commuting structure, mediated by the ancilla drive, creates an effective **dynamical generator** on the system that goes beyond the static $J_z^S$ encoding. The back-action of the driven ancilla through the Ising interaction is hypothesised to increase the effective generator's range, enabling $\Delta\theta < 1/T_H$.

**Key contrast with prior work**: Previous ancilla-assisted reports (2026-05-12, 2026-05-15) used $H_A = 0$ (passive ancilla) and the full four-term interaction $H_{\text{int}} = \sum_{ij} \alpha_{ij} J_i^S \otimes J_j^A$. Here $H_A \neq 0$ provides active ancilla dynamics, while the interaction is restricted to Ising-type $J_z \otimes J_z$ only. This is a fundamentally different regime: the ancilla drive precesses $J_z^A$ into $J_x^A$ and $J_y^A$ components, creating effective cross-couplings that were previously explicit in $H_{\text{int}}$ but are now dynamically generated.

## 💻 Numerical Simulation

### Implementation Strategy

1. **Operator construction** — Build $J_z^S$, $J_z^A$, $J_x^S$, $J_x^A$, $J_y^S$, $J_y^A$ as $4\times4$ Kronecker products from Pauli matrices, reusing the existing `build_two_qubit_operators()` in `src.physics.ancilla_optimization`. Construct $H_A$ as the linear combination $a_x J_x^A + a_y J_y^A + a_z J_z^A$. Construct $H_{\text{int}} = a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A$ (note: only one coefficient, not four). The total hold Hamiltonian is $H = \theta J_z^S + H_A + H_{\text{int}}$.

2. **State preparation** — The initial state $\vert00\rangle = \vert1,0\rangle_S \otimes \vert1,0\rangle_A$ is the first computational basis vector $[1, 0, 0, 0]^T$.

3. **Beam-splitter unitaries** — The standard 50/50 symmetric BS is $U_{\text{BS}} = \exp(-i \pi/2 J_x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(\mathbb{1}_2 - i \sigma_x)$. This acts only on the system: $U_{\text{BS}}^{(S)} = U_{\text{BS}} \otimes \mathbb{1}_2$.

4. **Hold unitary** — Compute $U_{\text{hold}}(T_H) = \exp(-i T_H H)$ via `scipy.linalg.expm`, fast and exact for $4\times4$ matrices. This is a single exponential of the full $H$, not a Trotter expansion.

5. **Sensitivity computation** — Compute $\langle J_z^S \rangle$ and $\text{Var}(J_z^S)$ via vector-matrix-vector products on the pure final state. Compute $\partial\langle J_z^S\rangle / \partial\theta$ via central finite differences with $\delta = 10^{-6}$, re-evaluating the full circuit at $\theta \pm \delta$.

6. **Optimisation** — The objective is $f(a_x, a_y, a_z, a_{xx}) = \Delta\theta$ at fixed $T_H = 10$ to be minimised. Since the initial state and BS settings are fixed (not optimised), the parameter space is 4-dimensional. Use a two-stage approach: (a) 2D slice scans and a 4D random search over $(a_x, a_y, a_z, a_{xx})$ to identify promising regions, then (b) local refinement via Nelder--Mead from the best random-search points.

7. **Store optimal parameters** — For each $\theta$ value, record the full tuple of optimal parameters $(a_x^*, a_y^*, a_z^*, a_{xx}^*)$ together with the achieved $\Delta\theta(\theta)$, the expectation $\langle J_z^S\rangle$, the variance $\text{Var}(J_z^S)$, and the derivative $\partial\langle J_z^S\rangle/\partial\theta$. These per-$\theta$ optimal configurations are stored for later analysis of how the optimal drive depends on the unknown phase.

- **2D slices**: Vary $(a_x, a_{xx})$ and $(a_y, a_{xx})$ on 11x11 grids with $\theta$ fixed at each of the 5 values (2 slices $\times$ 121 points $\times$ 5 $\theta$ values = 1210 evaluations).

- **Local refinement**: Nelder--Mead from the best 50 random-search points per $\theta$ value.
- **Record keeping**: For each $\theta$, the optimal parameters $(a_x^*, a_y^*, a_z^*, a_{xx}^*)$ and resulting $\Delta\theta$ are stored to characterise the $\theta$-dependence of the optimal drive.

### Validation

The following physical invariants are verified throughout every simulation run:

- **State normalisation**: $\|\vert\Psi_0\rangle\| = 1$ and $\|\vert\Psi_{\text{final}}\rangle\| = 1$ hold to machine precision.
- **Unitarity**: $U_{\text{BS}}^\dagger U_{\text{BS}} = \mathbb{1}_2$ (beam-splitter) and $U_{\text{hold}}^\dagger U_{\text{hold}} = \mathbb{1}_4$ (hold unitary).
- **Variance positivity**: $\text{Var}(J_z^S) \geq 0$, with numerical round-off clamped to zero when below $10^{-12}$.
- **Sensitivity positivity**: $\Delta\theta > 0$ for all valid configurations.
- **Baseline recovery**: At $a_{xx}=0$ and $H_A=0$, the circuit reduces to a single-qubit MZI with $\vert1,0\rangle$ input and 50/50 beam splitters. The sensitivity must satisfy $\Delta\theta = 1/T_H = 0.1$ to within relative tolerance $10^{-6}$.
- **Commutation relation**: $[J_z^S, J_x^S] = i J_y^S$ is verified to machine precision.
- **Hermiticity**: Both $H_A$ and $H_{\text{int}}$ satisfy $H^\dagger = H$.

## ⚠️ Expected Failure Conditions

| Failure | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| **SQL bound holds** — No combination of $(a_x, a_y, a_z, a_{xx})$ yields $\Delta\theta < 1/T_H$; the spectral radius of the effective generator remains bounded by $J=1/2$. | Accept the negative result; it confirms the analytical expectation that even a driven ancilla cannot increase the system's effective angular momentum. |
| **Optimal at decoupled limit** — The optimiser converges to $a_{xx} = 0$ and/or $a_x = a_y = 0$, implying the ancilla drive does not provide metrological benefit. | Report whether the optimum is at the boundary of the allowed parameter region; compare the best sensitivity at finite drive with the decoupled baseline. |
| **Fringe extremum** — The derivative $\partial\langle J_z^S\rangle / \partial\theta$ vanishes for certain parameter combinations, causing $\Delta\theta \to \infty$. | Detect and exclude configurations with $|\partial\langle J_z^S\rangle / \partial\theta| < 10^{-12}$; report fringe-extremum flags. |
| **Non-commuting structure is too weak** — For small $a_{xx}$ or small drive magnitudes, the effective time-dependent Hamiltonian is approximately commuting, and no SQL violation occurs. | Explicitly scan large $a_{xx}$ and large drive amplitudes. If even at the boundary of the scan range no improvement is seen, conclude the mechanism is ineffective. |
| **Optimisation landscape is flat** — Large regions of parameter space give $\Delta\theta \approx 1/T_H$, making it difficult to identify the true optimum. | Use many random restarts (500+) and report the histogram of $\Delta\theta$ values; compare the best 1%, median, and worst results. |
| **$a_z$ drive alone dominates** — If the optimiser converges to large $a_z$ with $a_x = a_y = 0$, the ancilla drive commutes with $J_z^A$ and no time-dependence is generated. | This is a legitimate negative result: a commuting ancilla drive provides no benefit, confirming Claim 3 of the hypothesis (non-commuting drive is essential). |
| **Phase-dependent sensitivity** — The optimal drive parameters may depend on the true $\theta$, which is unknown. | Evaluate sensitivity at several $\theta$ values and report the worst-case; discuss adaptive sensing strategies if the optimal is $\theta$-dependent. |

## ⚖️ Analytical Bounds

For the decoupled case ($a_{xx} = 0$), the system and ancilla evolve separately: the system undergoes a standard single-qubit MZI (BS $\to$ $\theta$ phase $\to$ BS) giving $\Delta\theta = 1/T_H$, while the ancilla evolves under $H_A$ independently and its dynamics have no effect on the $J_z^S$ measurement. This recovers the SQL.

When $a_{xx} \neq 0$ but $H_A = 0$ (passive ancilla), the total Hamiltonian is $H = \theta J_z^S + a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A$. Since $[J_z^S, J_z^S \otimes J_z^A] = 0$, the evolution factorises: $U_{\text{hold}} = \exp(-i T_H \theta J_z^S) \exp(-i T_H a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A)$. The second term creates S-A entanglement but commutes with $J_z^S$, so the $J_z^S$ measurement on the system is unaffected: $\Delta\theta = 1/T_H$ still holds. This is consistent with the prior report (2026-05-12) that found no improvement with a passive ancilla.

When $H_A \neq 0$ and $a_{xx} \neq 0$, the situation is more complex. The total Hamiltonian does not commute with itself at different times when $a_x \neq 0$ or $a_y \neq 0$ because $J_z^A$ evolves under $H_A$. However, there is an important **unitary equivalence**: transform into the interaction picture of $H_A$ via $V(t) = \exp(i t H_A)$. The total unitary can be written as:
$$U_{\text{hold}}(T_H) = e^{-i T_H H} = e^{-i T_H (H_S + H_A + H_{\text{int}})}.$$
Defining the interaction-picture operators $\tilde{H}_S(t) = e^{i t H_A} H_S e^{-i t H_A} = \theta J_z^S$ (since $H_A$ acts only on the ancilla) and $\tilde{H}_{\text{int}}(t) = e^{i t H_A} H_{\text{int}} e^{-i t H_A} = a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes e^{i t H_A} J_z^A e^{-i t H_A}$, the time-ordered evolution is:
$$U_{\text{hold}}(T_H) = e^{-i T_H H_A} \mathcal{T} \exp\left(-i \int_0^{T_H} [\tilde{H}_S(t) + \tilde{H}_{\text{int}}(t)] dt\right).$$
The pre-factor $e^{-i T_H H_A}$ acts purely on the ancilla and does not affect $J_z^S$ measurements. The time-ordered integral contains the non-commuting structure that could — in principle — enhance the sensitivity. The key question is whether the time-dependent generator $G(t) = \theta J_z^S + a_{xx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A(t)$ can generate a **net effective rotation** on the system with a larger effective angular momentum range than $J_z^S$ alone.

The **available analytical bound**: The system's $J_z^S$ operator has eigenvalues $\pm 1/2$, and any unitary generated by $J_z^S \otimes (\text{ancilla operator})$ cannot increase the eigenvalue range of the effective generator acting on the system's reduced state. The interaction-picture generator is $G(t) = J_z^S \otimes [\theta \mathbb{1}_2 + a_{xx} J_z^A(t)]$, where the ancilla operator $B(t) = \theta \mathbb{1}_2 + a_{xx} J_z^A(t)$ has eigenvalues depending on $\theta$ and the instantaneous $J_z^A(t)$ eigenvalues ($\pm 1/2$). Since $J_z^A$ is bounded by $\pm 1/2$, $B(t)$ has eigenvalues $\theta \pm a_{xx}/2$. The full $G(t)$ has eigenvalues $\pm \frac12 (\theta \pm a_{xx}/2)$, and the time-ordering can — in principle — generate phases that are **not achievable by a static Hamiltonian** of the same form. This is the potential loophole the hypothesis aims to test numerically.

The SQL bound $\Delta\theta \geq 1/T_H$ is derived from the QFI for a single qubit. However, the effective evolution in our case is not a simple unitary rotation on the system alone — the interaction with the ancilla correlates $J_z^S$ with $J_z^A(t)$, and the back-action from the driven ancilla can, in principle, induce amplitude changes (not just phase shifts) in the system, potentially making the measurement more sensitive to $\theta$. The numerical exploration will determine whether this back-action is sufficient to beat the $1/T_H$ bound.
