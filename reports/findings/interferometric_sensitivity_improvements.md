# Interferometric Sensitivity Improvements: Complete Experimental Record

## Overall Interferometric Scheme

All experiments use variants of a **Mach–Zehnder interferometer (MZI)** on two-mode bosonic systems (single-particle or multi-particle) with optional ancilla subsystems. The generic protocol is:

1. **Initial state preparation** — Typically a Fock state $|1,0\rangle$ (one particle in mode 0, vacuum in mode 1), Dicke state, coherent state, or a superposition generated by a 50/50 beam splitter $U_{\text{BS}} = \exp(-i\frac{\pi}{4}(a_0^\dagger a_1 + a_1^\dagger a_0))$.

2. **Holding period** — A Hamiltonian $H = H_S + H_A + H_{\text{int}}$ is applied for time $T_H$, where:
   - $H_S = \theta J_z^S$ encodes the unknown phase rate $\theta$
   - $H_A = \theta J_z^A$ in symmetric-encoding schemes (or $H_A = \theta(a_x J_x^A + a_y J_y^A + a_z J_z^A)$ in phase-modulated drive protocols)
   - $H_{\text{int}}$ is a system–ancilla coupling (Ising $J_z\otimes J_z$, XX, or general bilinear $\alpha_{ij} J_i^S \otimes J_j^A$)
   - Some protocols add a drive Hamiltonian $H_A = a_x J_x^A + a_y J_y^A + a_z J_z^A$ (not exposed to $\theta$) on the ancilla

3. **Readout** — Projective measurement of either $J_z^S$ on the system alone (ancilla traced out), or a joint measurement $M = a J_z^S + b J_z^A$ (weighted sum on both subsystems with $a^2+b^2=1$).

4. **Sensitivity metric** — Error-propagation uncertainty $\Delta\theta = \sqrt{\text{Var}(M)} / |\partial\langle M\rangle/\partial\theta|$, compared against the **standard quantum limit (SQL)** $\Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}} = 1/(\sqrt{N}\, T_H)$ for $N$ particles (or $1/(\sqrt{2N}\, T_H)$ when both S and A are measured jointly).

The default baseline is a single-particle ($N=1$) MZI with system-only phase encoding, 50/50 BS on S only, decoupled ancilla ($H_{\text{int}} = 0$), and $J_z^S$ measurement, which exactly saturates $\Delta\theta = 1/T_H$.

## List of Experiments

### 1. Single-Particle MZI: Holding-Time Scaling (2026-05-12)

- **Configuration**: No ancilla; single-particle MZI with $H = \theta J_z$, holding time $T_H$ swept from $10^{-2}$ to $10^3$.
- **Interferometric improvement**: None — confirms $\Delta\theta = 1/T_H$ (SQL) exactly. Scaling exponent $\alpha = -1.0$ (log-log slope).
- **Unexpected findings**: Analytical and finite-difference derivatives agree to machine precision. At fringe extrema ($\sin(\theta T_H) = 0$) the sensitivity diverges as expected.
- **Explanation**: Single-particle MZI without entanglement cannot beat the SQL; provides the essential baseline reference.

---

### 2. Scaling Survey: Fock-Basis MZI Models (2026-05-11)

- **Configuration**: Two-mode Fock space truncated at $N_{\max}$; various input states (coherent, NOON, squeezed vacuum); Markovian decoherence channels (phase diffusion, single-body loss, two-body loss).
- **Interferometric improvement**: Coherent states always achieve SQL ($\alpha=-0.5$). Squeezed-vacuum injection achieves Heisenberg-like scaling ($\alpha \to -1.0$) at large $N$ with $F_Q = 2\langle N\rangle(\langle N\rangle+1)$, beating the NOON-state HL prefactor by $2\times$.
- **Unexpected findings**: NOON states collapse to SQL ($\alpha \to -0.5$) under any loss; two-body loss causes complete collapse ($\alpha \to 0$) for all states at large $N$.
- **Explanation**: Squeezed vacuum has higher QFI than NOON at fixed photon number due to correlations between modes. Loss destroys the entanglement advantage.

---

### 3. Scaling Survey: Collective-Spin / Dicke Basis (2026-05-11)

- **Configuration**: Symmetric Dicke subspace ($J = N/2$) with OAT spin squeezing, balanced Dicke superpositions, non-Gaussian hybrid states, and ancilla-assisted protocols.
- **Interferometric improvement**: OAT achieves $\alpha = -2/3$ at optimal $t_{\text{opt}} \propto N^{-1/3}$. Balanced Dicke (Twin-Fock) achieves $\alpha = -1.0$ (Heisenberg) with $F_Q = N(N+2)/3$.
- **Unexpected findings**: Non-Gaussian states have fractional exponents $\alpha \approx -0.75$ to $-0.85$. Ancilla-assisted protocols preserve $\alpha$ but improve the prefactor $C$.
- **Explanation**: Spin squeezing interpolates between SQL and HL; Dicke superpositions saturate the HL in the symmetric subspace.

---

### 4. Ancilla-Assisted Metrology: Full-Parameter Optimization (2026-05-12)

- **Configuration**: System–ancilla pair ($N=1$ each) with general bilinear interaction $H_{\text{int}} = \alpha_{ij} J_i^S \otimes J_j^A$ ($i,j \in \{x,z\}$) and Nelder–Mead optimization over all parameters. S-only MZI.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **None** — the optimal configuration always $\alpha_{ij} = 0$, recovering the SQL. No configuration beats $\Delta\theta = 1/T_H$.
- **Unexpected findings**: Non-zero $\alpha_{ij}$ generally degrades sensitivity when measuring $J_z^S$ alone. The optimum is the trivial decoupled configuration.
- **Explanation**: The $J_z^S$ measurement cannot access the ancilla's share of the distributed phase information. Information flows from S to A via the interaction but is lost at readout.

---

### 5. Ancilla-Assisted Metrology with Joint Measurement (2026-05-15)

- **Configuration**: Same system–ancilla pair ($N=1$ each), general bilinear interaction, but now **joint measurement** $M = J_z^S + J_z^A$ (equal-weight sum) instead of $J_z^S$ alone.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Sub-SQL impossible** — achieves $\Delta\theta = 1/T_H$ (SQL) exactly at $\alpha=0$; for $\alpha \neq 0$, joint measurement recovers sensitivity that S-only measurement loses.
- **Unexpected findings**: The joint measurement never beats the SQL — it at best restores the SQL bound $F_Q \leq T_H^2$ (QFI bound).
- **Explanation**: $\theta$ couples only to S subsystem; QFI is bounded by $T_H^2$ regardless of measurement. Joint measurement recovers distributed information but cannot exceed the fundamental bound.

---

### 6. Weighted Joint Measurement: Generalization to N-M Particles (2026-05-18)

- **Configuration**: $N$ system particles, $M$ ancilla particles; bilinear interaction; **optimized weighted** joint measurement $M = a J_z^S + b J_z^A$ with $a^2+b^2=1$.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Beats the $N$-particle SQL** — scaling $\Delta\theta \propto 1/(N^\nu T_H)$ with $\nu > 1/2$, approaching $\nu \to 1$ (Heisenberg) for large $N$ with optimized weights.
- **Unexpected findings**: The optimal weight ratio $a^*/b^*$ varies with $\alpha_{ij}$, $N$, $M$, and $T_H$, determined by the relative variances and covariance of $J_z^S$ and $J_z^A$. Ancilla size $M$ matters: larger $M$ increases the variance pool. The $2N$-particle SQL ($1/(\sqrt{2N}\,T_H)$) is the relevant benchmark when both S and A are measured.
- **Explanation**: Weighted measurement extracts information from both subsystems; optimal weighting minimizes the error-propagation expression by balancing the signal derivative (scaling with $N$ via $J_z^S$) against the total variance (benefiting from $M$ ancilla particles).

---

### 7. Ancilla-Drive-Enhanced Metrology (2026-05-18)

- **Configuration**: Single-particle S and A, **drive Hamiltonian** on ancilla $H_A = a_x J_x^A + a_y J_y^A + a_z J_z^A$ (controllable, not exposed to $\theta$), Ising interaction $H_{\text{int}} = a_{zz} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A$, measurement $J_z^S$ on system. S-only MZI.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **None** — no configuration of $(a_x, a_y, a_z, a_{zz})$ beats the SQL.
- **Unexpected findings**: The drive-independent result contradicted the hypothesis that an actively driven ancilla could enable SQL violation via effective time-dependent Hamiltonians.
- **Explanation**: The system measurement $J_z^S$ cannot access the ancilla's driven dynamics. The drive creates ancilla coherence that is invisible to the S-only measurement. This motivated the drive-component analysis (2026-05-27) which confirmed that all 2D slices $(a_z,a_{zz})$, $(a_x,a_{zz})$, $(a_y,a_{zz})$ achieve at best $\Delta\theta/\text{SQL} = 1.0$.

---

### 8. Ancilla-Drive Phase-Modulated Metrology (2026-05-19)

- **Configuration**: Same as (7) but **ancilla drive is also exposed to $\theta$**: $H_A = \theta(a_x J_x^A + a_y J_y^A + a_z J_z^A)$.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Strong SQL violation** — best $\Delta\theta = 0.02036$, which is **$4.91\times$ below the SQL** ($\Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}} = 0.1$ at $T_H=10$).
- **Unexpected findings**: The violation is highly sensitive to the true $\theta$ (best at small $\theta \approx 0.2$). No violation occurs when $a_{zz} = 0$ even with drive. The dominant mechanism is a **static BCH cross-term** $[\theta J_z^S, a_{zz} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A]$ that generates a $J_z^A$ contribution in the effective Hamiltonian, not the direct time-dependent drive.
- **Explanation**: Phase modulation gives $\partial H_A/\partial\theta$ contributions to the derivative, increasing the slope. The cross-term between $H_S$ and $H_{\text{int}}$ transforms the Ising interaction into an effective $\theta J_z^A$ term on the ancilla, enabling sub-SQL sensitivity even with $N=1$.

---

### 9. XX-Coupling Ancilla Metrology (2026-05-20)

- **Configuration**: Both S and A couple to $\theta$ ($H_S = \theta J_z^S$, $H_A = \theta J_z^A$ — symmetric phase encoding); transverse coupling $H_{\text{int}} = \alpha_{xx} J_x^S \otimes J_x^A$; S-only MZI (BS only on S); measurement $J_z^S$ after tracing out ancilla.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **None** — $\alpha_{xx} = 0$ is always optimal for all $\theta$ and $\alpha_{xx} \in [0,20]$.
- **Unexpected findings**: Despite the ancilla now feeling $\theta$ directly ($H_A = \theta J_z^A$), the $J_z^S$ measurement cannot extract the ancilla's phase information because the $J_x^S \otimes J_x^A$ interaction does not correlate $J_z^S$ with $J_z^A$ in a way that a trace-out can recover.
- **Explanation**: The XX coupling creates transverse entanglement, which is destroyed when tracing out the ancilla. The $J_z^S$ measurement sees only the marginal system state, which retains no $\theta$ information from the ancilla.

---

### 10. General-Interaction Ancilla Metrology (2026-05-21)

- **Configuration**: Symmetric phase encoding ($H_S = \theta J_z^S$, $H_A = \theta J_z^A$), **full four-parameter interaction** $(\alpha_{xx}, \alpha_{xz}, \alpha_{zx}, \alpha_{zz})$, L-BFGS-B optimization instead of grid scans. S-only MZI.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Modest SQL violation** — best $\Delta\theta = 0.0690$, which is **$0.690\times$ the SQL** ($\Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}} = 0.10$), achieved at $\theta = 3.8$.
- **Unexpected findings**: The dominant optimal parameters are the inactive couplings $\alpha_{zx}$ and $\alpha_{zz}$ (mean magnitudes 6.22 and 6.37), not the transverse $\alpha_{xx}$ or $\alpha_{xz}$. These generate the SQL violation through **higher-order BCH cross-terms** with $H_0 = \theta(J_z^S + J_z^A)$. The protocol works at large $\theta$ (near $\pi$) where the BCH expansion produces sizable effective ancilla terms.
- **Explanation**: The cross-terms $[\theta J_z^S, \alpha_{zz} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A]$ and $[\theta J_z^A, \alpha_{zx} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A]$ generate effective $J_z^A$ contributions that transfer phase information to the ancilla; this is the same mechanism as the phase-modulated drive protocol (2026-05-19) but realized statically via large coupling constants rather than explicit drive.

---

### 11. Multi-Particle XX Coupling, Dual MZI (2026-05-22)

- **Configuration**: Symmetric phase encoding; $N \in [1,20]$ particles in both S and A; **dual MZI** (50/50 BS on both S and A before and after hold); $H_{\text{int}} = \alpha_{xx} J_x^S \otimes J_x^A$ only; measurement $J_z^S$ after tracing out ancilla.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **None** — $\alpha_{xx}^* = 0$ for all $(\theta, N)$. No SQL violation at any $N$.
- **Unexpected findings**: Even with multi-particle subsystems and both subsystems undergoing MZI sequences, the pure XX coupling cannot produce an advantage when the ancilla is traced out.
- **Explanation**: The dual MZI creates initial superposition in both S and A, but the $J_z^S$ marginal measurement discards ancilla information. The $J_x^S \otimes J_x^A$ interaction creates correlations in the $J_x$ basis, which are invisible to $J_z$ readout after the trace.

---

### 12. Four-Parameter Coupling, Multi-Particle Dual MZI (2026-05-23)

- **Configuration**: Symmetric phase encoding; $N \in [1,10]$ particles; **dual MZI**; **full four-parameter interaction** $(\alpha_{xx}, \alpha_{xz}, \alpha_{zx}, \alpha_{zz})$; measurement $J_z^S$ after tracing out ancilla. L-BFGS-B optimization.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Weak SQL violation** — best ratio $\Delta\theta/\text{SQL}_{\text{SQL}} = 0.916$ (SQL $\Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}} = 0.1/\sqrt{N}$).
- **Unexpected findings**: The improvement is much weaker than the $N=1$ single-MZI case (0.690$\times$SQL). This is anomalous: increasing $N$ and adding a dual MZI should provide more avenues for enhancement. The root cause is that the dual MZI **commutes the beam-splitters** with the coupling, reducing cross-term generation.
- **Explanation**: With dual MZI, the effective interaction after the initial BS is transformed by $U_{\text{BS}}^\dagger H_{\text{int}} U_{\text{BS}}$, which mixes the coupling terms. The BCH cross-term mechanism that worked at $N=1$ (single MZI) is weakened because the beam-splitters symmetrically distribute the operators, suppressing the static cross-term generation.

---

### 13. Multi-Particle XX-Coupling Dual-MZI with Optimized Joint Measurement (2026-05-25)

- **Configuration**: Same as (11) but **joint measurement** $M = m_s J_z^S + m_a J_z^A$ with $m_s^2 + m_a^2 = 1$, **jointly optimized** $\alpha_{xx}$ and $(m_s, m_a)$ per $(\theta, N)$. Benchmark is $2N$-particle SQL $\Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}} = 1/(\sqrt{2N}\, T_H)$.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Strong SQL violation** — best ratio $\Delta\theta/\text{SQL}_{2N} = 0.509$ (SQL $\Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}} = 1/(\sqrt{2N} \cdot 10)$), achieved at $N=5$, $\theta=0.5$.
- **Unexpected findings**: The optimal measurement coefficients are always unequal ($m_s \ne m_a$), with the ancilla weight $m_a$ dominating at large $\alpha_{xx}$. The SQL ratio improves with $N$ up to $N\approx5$ then plateaus. The asymptotic scaling is $\Delta\theta \propto 1/N^{0.75}$ (between SQL $\alpha=-0.5$ and HL $\alpha=-1.0$).
- **Explanation**: The joint measurement extracts correlations created by the XX interaction. The weighted sum optimally balances the signal derivative (from both S and A, since both feel $\theta$) against the total variance. Multi-particle subsystems amplify the available variance pool.

---

### 14. Phase-Diffusion Robustness of the Phase-Modulated Drive Protocol (2026-05-24)

- **Configuration**: Takes the best-performing configuration from the phase-modulated drive protocol (2026-05-19) and adds phase diffusion noise $\gamma_\phi$ on both qubits, then re-optimizes parameters.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Sub-SQL advantage survives finite noise** — the protocol maintains $\Delta\theta < \Delta\theta_{\text{SQL}}$ for $\gamma_\phi < \gamma_\phi^*$, with $\gamma_\phi^* \approx 0.08$ at $\theta=0.2$ and $\gamma_\phi^* \approx 0.12$ at $\theta=1.0$.
- **Unexpected findings**: The optimal drive amplitudes $(a_x, a_y, a_z)$ decrease with $\gamma_\phi$, while the interaction $\alpha_{zz}$ increases to compensate. The protocol is more fragile at small $\theta$ (where the noise-free advantage is largest) and more robust at larger $\theta$.
- **Explanation**: Phase diffusion decoheres the ancilla, reducing the effectiveness of the parametric amplification. The optimizer trades off drive strength for noise resilience; the static BCH cross-term (via $\alpha_{zz}$) becomes relatively more important as the drive is turned down.

---

### 15. Drive-Component Analysis in Ancilla-Enhanced Metrology (2026-05-27)

- **Configuration**: Back to the original driven-ancilla protocol (2026-05-18) with system-only MZI, $N=1$, measurement $J_z^S$. Systematic 2D slice analysis of $(a_z,a_{zz})$, $(a_x,a_{zz})$, $(a_y,a_{zz})$ landscapes with norm-constrained optimization.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **None confirmed** — all 2D slices achieve at best $\min \Delta\theta/\text{SQL} = 1.0$ (SQL). Non-commuting drives ($a_x$, $a_y$) produce qualitatively more complex landscapes but never dip below SQL.
- **Unexpected findings**: The commuting $(a_z,a_{zz})$ slice has a simple, structured landscape. The non-commuting $(a_x,a_{zz})$ and $(a_y,a_{zz})$ slices have non-trivial topography (ridges, valleys) but the global minimum is always at zero drive. Larger drive norm $\|\mathbf{a}\|$ does not enable sub-SQL sensitivity.
- **Explanation**: Without the ancilla also feeling $\theta$ (symmetric encoding or phase modulation), the driven ancilla dynamics are invisible to $J_z^S$ measurement. This confirms that the BCH cross-term mechanism requires $\theta$ in the ancilla Hamiltonian to generate signal information on the ancilla.

### 16. Ancilla-Assisted vs. Two-System-Particle Comparison (2026-05-11)

- **Configuration**: Fixed 2 total particles — Configuration A (1 system particle + 1 ancilla) vs. Configuration B (2 system particles). QFI optimization with full 4-parameter interaction $(\alpha_{xx}, \alpha_{xz}, \alpha_{zx}, \alpha_{zz})$. S-only MZI.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Ancilla-assisted cannot beat two-system-particle** — ratio $\mathcal{R} = \Delta\theta_A / \Delta\theta_B = 2.02$.
- **Unexpected findings**: Non-commuting interactions ($\alpha_{xz}, \alpha_{xx} \neq 0$) provide no QFI enhancement for the ancilla configuration, despite breaking $[J_z, H_{\text{int}}] \neq 0$ and generating additional contributions to the effective generator.
- **Explanation**: Fundamental limitation — a $J=1/2$ probe has generator eigenvalue range bounded by $1$; entangling with an ancilla cannot make it behave like a $J=1$ probe. The integral $\int_0^1 J_z(s)\,ds$ cannot produce a generator with larger eigenvalue range than bare $J_z$. Particle count determines the achievable angular momentum.

### 17. Non-Markovian Bath with Ancilla Protection (2026-05-09)

- **Configuration**: Tripartite system (oscillator $\otimes$ spin ancilla $\otimes$ pseudomode bath); Lorentzian spectral density; pulsed ancilla entanglement; QFI preservation ratio $\mathcal{R}(T) = F_Q(T)/F_Q(0)$. Phase encoding on an oscillator (not an MZI protocol).
- **Interferometric improvement**: **Pending** — simulation infrastructure complete and validated (75 unit tests), results awaiting parameter sweeps.
- **Hypothesis**: Ancilla coupling can protect QFI against non-Markovian decoherence, with optimal coupling $g^*$ depending on bath correlation rate $\lambda$. Benefit grows as $\lambda \to 0$ (more non-Markovian). In the Markovian limit ($\lambda \to \infty$), ancilla provides diminishing returns.
- **Explanation**: The pseudomode representation exactly implements a Lorentzian bath. The ancilla is entangled with the oscillator before decoherence via $H_{\text{sa}} = g_{\text{sa}} (a^\dagger a) \otimes \sigma_x$; the entanglement stores phase information in spin-oscillator correlations that are more robust to bath noise. The single Lindblad dissipator $L_{\text{pm}} = \sqrt{\lambda}\,b$ on the pseudomode generates the exact non-Markovian dynamics.

### 19. Free Ancilla Initial State in Driven-Ancilla Metrology (2026-05-28)

- **Configuration**: System--ancilla pair ($N=1$ each) with system-only MZI (50/50 BS on S only), **free ancilla initial state** $|\psi_A\rangle = \cos(\theta_A/2)|1,0\rangle + e^{i\phi_A}\sin(\theta_A/2)|0,1\rangle$ (two additional DOF beyond the fixed-ancilla baseline), ancilla drive $H_A = a_x J_x^A + a_y J_y^A + a_z J_z^A$ (controllable, $\theta$-independent), Ising interaction $H_{\text{int}} = a_{zz} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A$, measurement $J_z^S$ on system. 6-parameter random search (2000 samples + 30 NM refinements per $\theta$) across all four scenarios.
- **Interferometric improvement**: **None** — no configuration of $(\theta_A, \phi_A, a_x, a_y, a_z, a_{zz})$ beats the SQL. All four scenarios (A: fixed ancilla baseline, B: free ancilla + drive + interaction, C: free ancilla + interaction only, D: free ancilla + drive only) converge to exactly $\Delta\theta = 0.1$ ($\Delta\theta \times T_H = 1$) for all five $\theta$ values. The 2D slice $(\theta_A, a_{zz})$ with $H_A=0$ is flat at SQL across 40,401 grid points — zero points below SQL at any $\theta$.
- **Unexpected findings**: Even though the ancilla starts in an arbitrary superposition (not a $J_z^A$ eigenstate), which immediately creates S--A entanglement via $H_{\text{int}}$ at $t=0$, the sensitivity remains exactly at the SQL. The optimal $\theta_A^*$ varies widely across $\theta$ values (from $0.65$ rad to $2.99$ rad), demonstrating that the optimiser explores non-trivial ancilla states — yet none produce sub-SQL sensitivity. The free ancilla does not converge to the fixed-ancilla limit $\theta_A=0$; the $J=1/2$ bound acts as a global floor, not a local optimum.
- **Explanation**: The $J=1/2$ spectral radius bound is absolute for product initial states. The $\theta$-modulated drive mechanism (2026-05-19) remains the only demonstrated route to SQL violation in this architecture because it generates $\partial H_A/\partial\theta$ contributions via parametric amplification. Freeing the ancilla initial state while keeping the drive $\theta$-independent cannot circumvent the fundamental bound — the generator $J_z^S$ has a fixed eigenvalue range of $1$, and no product-state ancilla configuration can increase it. This closes the loop on the question: for the $\theta$-independent ancilla drive protocol, no additional degree of freedom (ancilla state, drive components, interaction strength) enables sub-SQL sensitivity with a $J_z^S$ measurement.

## Two Most Captivating Enhancement Mechanisms

**1. Phase-modulated ancilla drive (Experiment 8):** Achieves $4.91\times$ below the SQL using a single system particle and a single ancilla, via a static BCH cross-term between $\theta J_z^S$ and an Ising coupling $a_{zz} J_z^S \otimes J_z^A$, combined with parametric amplification from exposing the drive to $\theta$. Shows surprising robustness to phase diffusion up to $\gamma_\phi \approx 0.08$.

**2. XX coupling with optimized weighted joint measurement (Experiment 13):** Reaches $0.509\times$ the $2N$-particle SQL at $N=5$, with asymptotic scaling $\Delta\theta \propto 1/N^{0.75}$ (between SQL $\alpha=-0.5$ and HL $\alpha=-1.0$). Achieved by jointly optimizing both the XX coupling strength and the measurement weights $(m_s, m_a)$ so that the weighted sum extracts information from both subsystems. Multi-particle subsystems amplify the variance pool available to the weighted measurement.
